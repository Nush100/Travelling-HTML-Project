Galle station

Galle is a city on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka. It is known for Galle Fort, the fortified old city founded by Portuguese colonists in the 16th century. Stone sea walls, expanded by the Dutch, encircle car-free streets with architecture reflecting Portuguese, Dutch and British rule. Notable buildings include the 18th-century Dutch Reformed Church. Galle Lighthouse stands on the fort's southeast tip.

District: Galle District

Provice: Southern Provice


Destinations to visit from Galle Station

  Galle Dutch Port

Galle Dutch Fort

Galle Dutch Fort is a heritage for Sri Lanka and it was built in 1588 by the Protuguese. It is situated in the bay of Galle on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka.

Many people visit Galle Dutch Fort when they travell to Galle. You can freely wander here and stay through out the day. This place relax minds with the ocean view.

  Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna is one of the biggest tourist destination in Sri Lanka and it is very famous amoung both local and foreign tourists. It is This is a lovely beach which has golden sand and surrounded by green palm trees.

Part of this beach is occupied with sunbeds for rent. At the west end of the beach there is a small hill with a pagoda and big buddha statue.

  Stilt Fishing

Stilt Fishing

Stilt Fishing is one of the most interesting traditional fishing method in Sri lanka. This fishermen use a vertical pole with an attached crossbar to sit and wait for fish. They use a rod to catch the fish.

This fishing method is not a commonly done. But in South coast you can experience this if you travell here. In unawatuna beach you can see those fishermen fishing silently and calmly.
