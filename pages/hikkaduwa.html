  Hikkaduwa Coral garden and Beach

Coral Garden

A coral reef is an underwater ecosystem characterized by reef-building corals. Reefs are formed of colonies of coral polyps held together by calcium carbonate. Most coral reefs are built from stony corals, whose polyps cluster in groups.

In the Hikkaduwa National Park, there are many corals which attrack the attention of both local and foreign travellers.

  Turtle Hatchery & Rescue Center

Turtle Hatchery & Rescue Center

Sea Turtle Hatchery & Rescue Centre is situated on Galle Road in Hikkaduwa. The sanctuary takes recently laid turtle eggs out of the beach and keeps them in a secured area until the baby turtles hatch. It is a must visit place to go with kids especially since one can experience every stage from eggs buried beneath the sand waiting to hatch to disabled turtles that the centre takes care of.

Distance from Hikkaduwa - 3.7km
