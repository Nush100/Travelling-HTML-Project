Beginning of Rail Way in Sri Lanka

British colonial government has introduced the rail network for Sri Lanka in 1864. The main reason behind introducing railway to Sri Lanka is to transport tea and coffee from the hill country to Colombo. The first road built with 54 kilometers connecting Colombo and Ambepussa. Then extennsions were made later to Kandy, Nawalapitiya, Nanu Oya, Bandarawela and Badulla.

Golden Age of Rail Way

The Golden age of Sri Lankan rail way was from 1955 to 1970 under the management of Mr.B D Rampala. He was the chief engineer of that period and later he was the general manager of the Ceylon Government Railway. he had upgraded major rail way stations outside colombo and rebuit the track in the Eastern Province. He has introduced the express trains. Untill 1953, steam locomotive had used in railways and during this golden age diesel locomotives introduced by Mr.Rampala.

Railway Museum In Sri Lanka

To cooemorate the 150th Anniversary of the first train run in Sri Lanka , The railways decided open a National Railway Museum. The National Railway Museum was started on 27th December 2014 at Kadugannawa.
